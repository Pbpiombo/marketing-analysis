# SQL Analysis Insights

## Question 1 — Average spending by child status

Customers without children have the highest average total spending and spend more across premium product categories, especially wine and meat.

This confirms that child status is one of the strongest drivers of customer value in this dataset.

---

## Question 2 — Purchase channel usage by child status

Store purchases are the dominant channel across all child-status groups.

Customers without children show higher catalog and store activity, while families with children rely more on deals. This suggests that no-child customers may be more responsive to premium and catalog-based campaigns, while family segments may be more price-sensitive.

---

## Question 3 — Purchase channel usage by age group

Store purchases are confirmed as the dominant channel across all age groups, followed by web purchases.

Elder customers show the highest activity across most channels, while Senior customers show the highest deal-related activity. This suggests that older customers should not be underestimated in digital and multi-channel strategies.

---

## Question 4 — Highest-value customer segment

Customers without children show higher average spending across all age groups.

The highest-value segment is made up of Senior customers without children, especially for wine and meat products. Elder customers also show strong average spending, particularly in wine.

This suggests that premium campaigns should prioritize no-child Senior and Elder customers.

---

## Question 5 — Campaign acceptance distribution

The majority of customers did not accept any campaign.

This shows that current campaigns are underperforming and that there is significant room for improvement through more targeted, segment-based marketing strategies.

---

## Question 6 — Campaign acceptance by segment

Some customer segments show stronger campaign acceptance rates than others.

However, acceptance rate should not be evaluated alone. It should be interpreted together with average spending to identify segments that are both responsive and commercially valuable.

---

## Question 7 — Product spending by age group

Product spending varies by age group, especially for premium categories such as wine and meat.

However, age alone is less informative than the combination of age group and child status. For this reason, the most useful interpretation comes from the segment-level analysis in Question 4.

---

## Question 8 — Complaints vs spending

Customers who complain show similar recency values compared to non-complainers, meaning they do not necessarily stop purchasing immediately.

However, they show much lower average spending and purchase activity. Since the number of customers who complained is very small, this result should be interpreted carefully, but it may still suggest that complaints are an early warning signal for reduced customer engagement.
